## 📖 Core Concept
In networking, **Media** refers to the physical material or medium used to transmit data (bits/ones and zeros) between network devices. It is not related to audio or video content. The three primary categories of media are:
* **Copper:** Electrical signals transmitted through metallic conductors (e.g., Cat6A cables).
* **Fiber:** Light pulses transmitted through glass or plastic strands (e.g., ISP connections).
* **Radio Frequency (RF) Waves:** Invisible electromagnetic waves used for wireless communication (e.g., Wi-Fi).

The curriculum focuses on **CompTIA Network+ Objective 1.5** (Compare and contrast transmission media and transceivers) and **Objective 5.5** (Given a scenario, use the appropriate tool or protocol to solve networking issues).

## 🛠️ Hardware & Devices
* **Copper Media:** Physical cables used for wired networking (e.g., Cat6A).
* **Fiber Media:** Optical cables used for high-speed, long-distance data transmission.
* **Connectors (Copper):**
 * **RJ45:** Registered Jack 45; standard connector for Ethernet networking.
 * **RJ11:** Registered Jack 11; typically used for telephone connections.
 * **F-Type:** Connector used for coaxial cables (often for cable TV/internet).
 * **BNC:** Bayonet Neill-Concelman; connector used for coaxial cables (often in older networks or video).
* **Connectors (Fiber):**
 * **SC:** Subscriber Connector.
 * **ST:** Straight Tip.
 * **LC:** Lucent Connector.
 * **MTRJ:** Mechanical Transfer Registered Jack.
 * **MTP:** Multi-fiber Termination Push-on.
* **Tools:**
 * **Cable Stripper:** Used to remove the outer jacket of a cable.
 * **Cable Crimper:** Used to attach connectors to the end of a cable.
 * **Cable Tester:** Used to verify the continuity and integrity of a cable.
* **Transceiver:** A device that combines both transmission and receiving capabilities into a single unit, often used to convert between different media types (e.g., fiber to copper).

## 🔑 Key Terminology
* **Media:** The physical material used to transmit data between devices in a network.
* **Copper Media:** A type of network media that uses electrical pulses over metallic wires.
* **Fiber Media:** A type of network media that uses light pulses over glass or plastic strands.
* **Radio Frequency (RF) Waves:** Electromagnetic waves used to carry data in wireless networks.
* **Transceiver:** A hardware component that can both transmit and receive data, often used as an interface module to convert signals between different media types.
* **Single-mode Fiber:** A type of fiber optic cable designed to carry a single mode of light, typically used for long distances.
* **Multi-mode Fiber:** A type of fiber optic cable designed to carry multiple modes of light, typically used for shorter distances.

## 🔄 How It Works (Processes)
* **Data Transmission:** Data is converted into ones and zeros, which are then encoded into physical signals (electrical, light, or RF) to be sent across the chosen media type.
* **Cable Creation:** The process involves stripping the outer jacket, arranging wires (if applicable), and using a crimper to secure the connector, followed by testing with a cable tester to ensure connectivity.
* **Media Conversion:** Transceivers act as the bridge between different physical media, allowing a device to accept an input from one medium (e.g., fiber) and output it to another (e.g., copper).

## 📊 Comparisons & Key Differences
| Media Type| Signal Method| Primary Use Case|
| :---| :---| :---|
| **Copper**| Electrical Pulses| Local Area Networks (LANs), Desktop connections|
| **Fiber**| Light Pulses| Long-distance, high-bandwidth (ISP, backbone)|
| **RF Waves**| Electromagnetic Waves| Wireless connectivity (Wi-Fi, mobile)|
