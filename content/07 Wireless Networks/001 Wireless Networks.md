## 📖 Core Concept
Wireless networks extend a physical, wired network into a wireless domain, providing mobility and convenience. They allow users to roam within a coverage area (e.g., across a college campus, in coffee shops, or even on airplanes) without being tethered to a physical port. The demand for wireless connectivity has grown from a niche technology in the late 1990s to a ubiquitous expectation in modern infrastructure.

This section covers:
* **Objective 1.5**: Comparing and contrasting transmission media and transceivers.
* **Objective 2.3**: Selecting and configuring wireless devices and technologies based on specific scenarios.

## 🛠️ Hardware & Devices
* **Wireless Antennas**: Used to transmit and receive radio frequency signals. Types include:
 * **Omnidirectional**: Radiates signal in all directions.
 * **Unidirectional**: Focuses signal in a specific direction.
 * **Yagi**: A specific type of directional antenna used for long-range or focused point-to-point communication.
* **Wireless Clients**: Devices such as laptops, smartphones, and tablets that connect to the wireless network.
* **Wireless Access Points (WAPs)**: Devices that act as the bridge between wireless clients and the wired network infrastructure.

## 📜 Protocols & Standards
* **IEEE 802.11 Standards**: The family of protocols defining WiFi technologies. The course covers:
 * **802.11a**
 * **802.11b**
 * **802.11g**
 * **802.11ac**
 * **802.11ax**
* **Wireless Frequencies**:
 * **2.4 GHz**
 * **5 GHz**
 * **6 GHz**
* **Security Protocols**:
 * **WEP** (Wired Equivalent Privacy)
 * **WPA** (Wi-Fi Protected Access)
 * **WPA2**
 * **WPA3**
 * **WPS** (Wi-Fi Protected Setup): A standard for simplifying the connection process between devices.

## 🔑 Key Terminology
* **Ad Hoc Mode**: A decentralized wireless network configuration where devices connect directly to each other without an access point.
* **Infrastructure Mode**: A wireless network configuration where devices connect to a central access point to communicate with the network.
* **Captive Portal**: A web page that requires user interaction (such as accepting terms or logging in) before granting access to the network; commonly used in public hotspots.
* **Aircrack-ng**: A suite of tools used for assessing WiFi network security, including monitoring, attacking, testing, and cracking passwords.
* **Penetration Tester**: A security professional who attempts to find and exploit vulnerabilities in a network to improve its security posture.

## 🔄 How It Works (Processes)
* **Wireless Configuration**: The process of setting up a SOHO (Small Office/Home Office) wireless network, including selecting security protocols and managing client connections.
* **Wireless Security Cracking**: An attacker uses tools (like Aircrack-ng) to capture wireless traffic and attempt to brute-force or crack the WiFi password to gain unauthorized access.
* **Captive Portal Authentication**: A user connects to an open or semi-secure network, is redirected by the WAP to a specific webpage, and must perform an action (login/agreement) before the WAP allows traffic to pass to the internet.

## 📊 Comparisons & Key Differences
* **Ad Hoc vs. Infrastructure Mode**:
 * **Ad Hoc**: Peer-to-peer, no central management.
 * **Infrastructure**: Client-to-Access Point, centralized management and connectivity to the wired backbone.
* **Antenna Types**:
 * **Omnidirectional**: Best for general coverage (e.g., a room).
 * **Unidirectional/Yagi**: Best for point-to-point or long-distance, focused coverage.
