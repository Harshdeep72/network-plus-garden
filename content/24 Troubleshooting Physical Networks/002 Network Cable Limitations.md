## 📖 Core Concept
This lesson covers the physical layer specifications, distance limitations, and environmental considerations for various network cabling media. Understanding these limitations is critical for network design, troubleshooting, and ensuring signal integrity.

## 🛠️ Hardware & Devices

### Twisted Pair (Copper)
* **CAT 5 (100BASE-TX):** 100 Mbps, up to 100 meters.
* **CAT 5e (1000BASE-T):** 1 Gbps, up to 100 meters.
* **CAT 6 (1000BASE-T / 10GBASE-T):** 1 Gbps up to 100m; 10 Gbps up to 55m.
* **CAT 6a / CAT 7 (10GBASE-T):** 10 Gbps, up to 100 meters.
* **CAT 8 (40GBASE-T):** 40 Gbps, up to 30 meters.

### Coaxial & Twinaxial
* **Coaxial:** Up to 100 Mbps, up to 500 meters (Ethernet).
* **Twinaxial (Standard):** Up to 10 Gbps, up to 5 meters.
* **Twinaxial (Newer):** Up to 100 Gbps, up to 7 meters.

### Fiber Optic
* **100BASE-FX:** 100 Mbps, up to 2 km (Multimode).
* **100BASE-SX:** 100 Mbps, up to 300 m (Multimode).
* **1000BASE-SX:** 1 Gbps, 220 m to 500+ m (Multimode).
* **1000BASE-LX:** 1 Gbps, 550 m (Multimode) or 5 km (Single-mode).
* **10GBASE-SR:** 10 Gbps, up to 400 m (Multimode).
* **10GBASE-LR:** 10 Gbps, up to 10 km (Single-mode).

## 📜 Protocols & Standards
* **TIA 568 A/B:** Standards for wiring pinouts. Crossover cables use 568-A on one end and 568-B on the other.
* **PoE (Power over Ethernet):** Technology for delivering electrical power over twisted pair data cables.
 * **Requirements:** Minimum CAT 5e.
 * **Power delivery:** 15.4W–60W (2 pairs); 60W–100W (4 pairs).

## 🔑 Key Terminology
* **EMI (Electromagnetic Interference):** Disturbance generated by an external source that affects an electrical circuit by electromagnetic induction or radiation.
* **STP (Shielded Twisted Pair):** Twisted pair cable with foil shielding around individual pairs and an overall foil layer to protect against EMI.
* **UTP (Unshielded Twisted Pair):** Standard twisted pair cable without shielding; inexpensive and flexible.
* **Plenum Rated:** Cable jacket material (Low smoke PVC or FEP) designed to be fire-retardant and low-toxic for use in HVAC/air-handling spaces.
* **Riser Rated:** Cable designed for vertical runs between floors; self-extinguishing to prevent fire from traveling upward.
* **Rollover/Console Cable:** A null-modem cable (typically flat/blue) used for out-of-band management of network devices.
* **Crossover Cable:** A cable used to connect two similar devices (e.g., switch-to-switch, PC-to-PC) directly.
* **Out-of-Band Communication:** Managing a device via a dedicated console port rather than through the network data path.

## 🔄 How It Works (Processes)
* **Plenum vs. Riser Selection:** Plenum cables can be used in both plenum and riser spaces. Riser cables **cannot** be used in plenum spaces due to toxic smoke risks during fire.
* **PoE Operation:** Data and power are transmitted simultaneously over the same copper pairs.
* **Crossover Pinouts:** By swapping the transmit (TX) and receive (RX) pairs (using A and B standards), the device's output is routed to the other device's input.

## 📊 Comparisons & Key Differences

| Feature| Shielded (STP)| Unshielded (UTP)|
| :---| :---| :---|
| **EMI Protection**| High| Low|
| **Cost**| Higher| Lower|
| **Flexibility**| Lower| Higher|
| **Use Case**| Factories, Airports| General LANs|

## ⚠️ Exam Tips & Important Notes
* **The 100m Rule:** Regardless of the twisted pair category, the maximum recommended length for data transmission is 100 meters.
* **Fiber Immunity:** Fiber is immune to EMI because it uses light, not electricity. If a site has extreme EMI, recommend fiber.
* **PoE Safety:** Never use less than CAT 5e for PoE, as it can be dangerous.
* **Crossover Usage:** Use crossover cables for like-to-like connections (e.g., Switch to Switch).
