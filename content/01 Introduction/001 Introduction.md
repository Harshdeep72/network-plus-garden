## 📖 Core Concept
This course provides a comprehensive foundation in networking, specifically designed for the [[CompTIA Network+]] (N10-009) certification exam. It aims to equip students with the knowledge and skills to configure, manage, and troubleshoot network infrastructure within various organizational settings. The certification is considered a foundational stepping stone for individuals entering the [[IT]] or [[cybersecurity]] industries.

The course builds upon assumed knowledge from the [[CompTIA A+]] certification (Core 1 and Core 2 exams), covering hardware, software, and basic computer networks. While A+ knowledge is beneficial, it is not a strict prerequisite, as essential A+ topics will be briefly reviewed if critical for Network+ understanding. The primary focus is on advancing from foundational knowledge to practical application in enterprise networks.

The course is structured to be a full textbook replacement, with an emphasis on practical, practitioner-led instruction from an instructor with over two decades of experience in IT and cybersecurity across organizations of varying sizes (from less than 50 people to those spanning six continents and millions of users).

The [[CompTIA Network+ N10-009]] exam covers five main domains, each contributing a specific percentage to the total exam questions:
* **Domain 1: Networking Concepts (23%)**: Covers fundamental topics like network topologies, protocols, and various networking components. Mastery is crucial as it forms the basis for configuration, management, and troubleshooting.
* **Domain 2: Network Implementation (20%)**: Assesses practical skills in setting up and configuring network infrastructure. Includes routing technologies (static/dynamic), switching technologies (VLANs, STP), wireless technologies (channel selection, encryption, authentication), and physical installation aspects (equipment placement, environmental considerations).
* **Domain 3: Network Operations (19%)**: Focuses on operational aspects of network management, including organizational documents, lifecycle management, network monitoring tools (SNMP, SIEMs), disaster recovery concepts (RPO, RTO), implementing network services (IPv4, IPv6), managing network access and performance, and using various protocols for network management.
* **Domain 4: Network Security (14%)**: The smallest domain, covering fundamental security concepts (encryption, IAM, common terminologies), physical and logical network security measures, network segmentation, and security policy enforcement.
* **Domain 5: Network Troubleshooting (24%)**: The largest domain, testing problem-solving skills, understanding troubleshooting methodology, resolving common cabling and interface issues, addressing service-related problems, performance issues (bandwidth, latency, packet loss), and using tools/protocols for diagnosis and resolution.

The course content is organized logically for optimal learning, not strictly by the order of objectives in the official CompTIA exam objectives PDF. Each section will clearly indicate which objectives and domains are being covered.

## 📜 Protocols & Standards
* **[[SNMP]] (Simple Network Management Protocol)**: A protocol used for network monitoring and management, mentioned as a tool covered in the Network Operations domain.
* **[[IPv4]] (Internet Protocol version 4)**: A core protocol for identifying devices on a network using a 32-bit address, mentioned in the context of implementing network services.
* **[[IPv6]] (Internet Protocol version 6)**: The latest version of the Internet Protocol, using 128-bit addresses, also mentioned in the context of implementing network services.
* **[[VLANs]] (Virtual Local Area Networks)**: A method of creating logically separate networks within a single physical network, covered under switching technologies in the Network Implementation domain.
* **[[STP]] (Spanning Tree Protocol)**: A network protocol that ensures a loop-free logical topology for Ethernet networks, covered under switching technologies in the Network Implementation domain.
* **[[OSI Reference Model]] (Open Systems Interconnection Reference Model)**: A conceptual framework that describes functions of a networking system in seven layers, explicitly mentioned as an objective (1.1) to explain its concepts and layers.

## 🔑 Key Terminology
* **[[CompTIA Network+]]**: A vendor-neutral certification that validates the foundational skills needed to securely establish, maintain, and troubleshoot essential networks.
* **[[CompTIA A+]]**: A foundational IT certification that covers hardware, software, and basic computer networks, considered assumed knowledge for Network+.
* **[[IT Operations and Administration]]**: The processes and activities involved in managing the day-to-day functions of an organization's information technology infrastructure.
* **[[Network Infrastructure]]**: The entire collection of hardware and software resources of an entire network that enable network connectivity, communication, operations, and management of an enterprise network.
* **[[Enterprise Networks]]**: Large, complex computer networks used by organizations to connect their various departments, offices, and systems, often spanning multiple locations.
* **[[Certification Exam]]**: A standardized test designed to validate an individual's knowledge and skills in a specific area, leading to a professional certification.
* **[[Domains]] (Exam Domains)**: The major subject areas or categories of knowledge that an exam tests, each with a specific weight (percentage of questions).
* **[[Objectives]] (Exam Objectives)**: Specific, measurable statements within each domain that describe what a candidate must be able to do or know for the exam (e.g., "explain," "compare and contrast," "implement," "analyze").
* **[[Network Topologies]]**: The arrangement of the elements (links, nodes, etc.) of a communication network, covered in Networking Concepts.
* **[[Protocols]]**: A set of rules that govern how data is transmitted and received between devices in a network.
* **[[Networking Components]]**: The various hardware and software elements that make up a network, such as routers, switches, cables, and network interface cards.
* **[[Routing Technologies]]**: Methods and protocols used to determine the best path for data packets to travel across networks, including static and dynamic routing.
* **[[Switching Technologies]]**: Methods and devices (like switches) used to forward data frames between devices within the same local area network (LAN).
* **[[Wireless Technologies]]**: Technologies that enable communication between devices without physical cables, including aspects like channel selection, encryption, and authentication.
* **[[Physical Needs and Aspects of Network Installation]]**: Considerations for the physical setup of a network, such as equipment placement, cabling, and environmental factors.
* **[[Organizational Documents]]**: Policies, procedures, and other formal records that guide network operations and management.
* **[[Lifecycle Management]]**: The process of managing the entire lifespan of network devices and systems, from procurement to retirement.
* **[[Network Monitoring Tools]]**: Software and hardware used to observe and track the performance, availability, and security of a network.
* **[[SIEMs]] (Security Information and Event Management)**: A solution that combines security information management (SIM) and security event management (SEM) functions into one security management system.
* **[[Disaster Recovery Concepts]]**: Strategies and procedures for restoring an organization's IT infrastructure and operations after a disruptive event.
* **[[RPO]] (Recovery Point Objective)**: The maximum acceptable amount of data loss measured in time.
* **[[RTO]] (Recovery Time Objective)**: The maximum acceptable length of time that a computer system, application, or network can be down after a disaster or disruption.
* **[[Network Services]]**: Applications and functions provided over a network, such as DNS, DHCP, and web services.
* **[[Network Access]]**: The methods and controls used to allow or restrict users and devices from connecting to a network.
* **[[Network Performance]]**: The measure of the quality of service of a network, often assessed by metrics like bandwidth, latency, and throughput.
* **[[Encryption]]**: The process of converting information or data into a code to prevent unauthorized access.
* **[[IAM]] (Identity and Access Management)**: A framework of policies and technologies for ensuring that the right users have the appropriate access to technology resources.
* **[[Physical Security Measures]]**: Controls designed to prevent unauthorized physical access to network devices and infrastructure.
* **[[Logical Security Measures]]**: Controls implemented in software and operating systems to protect data and systems from unauthorized access.
* **[[Network Segmentation]]**: The practice of dividing a computer network into multiple smaller network segments or subnets.
* **[[Security Policies]]**: Formal statements that define how security is implemented and managed within an organization.
* **[[Troubleshooting Methodology]]**: A systematic approach to identifying, diagnosing, and resolving problems in a network.
* **[[Cabling Issues]]**: Problems related to network cables, such as breaks, incorrect connections, or interference.
* **[[Interface Issues]]**: Problems related to network interface cards (NICs) or ports on network devices.
* **[[Service Related Problems]]**: Issues affecting network services, such as a DNS server not responding or a DHCP server failing to assign IP addresses.
* **[[Bandwidth]]**: The maximum rate of data transfer across a given path.
* **[[Latency]]**: The delay before a transfer of data begins following an instruction for its transfer.
* **[[Packet Loss]]**: When one or more packets of data traveling across a computer network fail to reach their destination.
* **[[Performance-Based Questions]] (PBQs)**: Exam questions that require candidates to perform simulated job functions in a virtual environment, often called simulations.
* **[[Multiple Choice Questions]]**: Standard exam questions where candidates select one correct answer from a list of options.
* **[[Multiple Select Questions]]**: Exam questions where candidates must select two or more correct answers from a list of options.
* **[[Exam Voucher]]**: A code purchased from CompTIA or an authorized partner that allows a candidate to register for and take a certification exam.
* **[[Scaled Score]]**: A raw score that has been converted to a standardized scale, used by CompTIA to report exam results.
* **[[Closed Captioning]]**: Text displayed on a video screen that provides a transcription of the audio, often used for accessibility.
* **[[Transcript]]**: A written record of all spoken words in a video or audio recording.
* **[[Study Guide]]**: A document, often in PDF format, that summarizes key information from a course for review and note-taking.
* **[[Facebook Group]]**: An online community on Facebook where members can interact, ask questions, and share information.
* **[[Discord Server]]**: A communication platform where users can interact via voice, video, and text in channels.
* **[[Q&A Portion]]**: A section within an online course platform where students can post questions and receive answers from instructors or support staff.

## 📊 Comparisons & Key Differences
* **CompTIA Network+ vs. CompTIA A+**:
 * **CompTIA A+**: Considered foundational knowledge covering hardware, software, and basic computer networks. Assumed knowledge for Network+.
 * **CompTIA Network+**: Builds upon A+ knowledge, focusing specifically on configuring, managing, and troubleshooting enterprise network infrastructure. Designed for beginners with less than one year of IT experience or those who have completed A+.
* **Course Content Order vs. CompTIA Exam Objectives PDF Order**:
 * **Course Content Order**: Designed for logical learning progression, starting with basics and building up to advanced topics and troubleshooting.
 * **CompTIA Exam Objectives PDF Order**: A checklist of topics for the exam, not necessarily structured for optimal teaching or learning flow. The course covers all objectives but not in the PDF's sequential order.
* **Multiple Choice vs. Multiple Select Questions**:
 * **Multiple Choice**: Select only one correct answer from the given options.
 * **Multiple Select**: Select two or three correct answers from the given options.
* **Small vs. Large Organizations (Instructor's Experience)**:
 * **Small Organizations (e.g., < 50 people)**: Instructor's own company, Dion Training.
 * **Large Organizations (e.g., spanning six continents, dozens of countries, millions of end users)**: One of the instructor's previous positions.
 * **Commonality**: Both types of organizations rely daily on their networks, requiring similar principles and techniques for configuration, management, operation, defense, and troubleshooting.
